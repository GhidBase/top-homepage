My Work

Lucky Defense Guides

I made this website as a passion project when I started The Odin Project. In lots of ways, it made TOP take much longer than it needed to. But I don't regret it. Lucky Defense Guides gave me time to practice what I learned, and made me do it in a way that was good enough for my users - as of today, LDG has had half a million views, and is even maintained by a team of 5 people! The community that's been built around this game has been truly inspiring and I can't wait to see it grow further.

FlipDecks

My first public app released on the Google Play Store. Users can log on, collect cards, and message other players. Using GoogleAuth, players can also pick right up where they left off on any other device and continue playing on the same account.

Binary Search Tree

Learning how to traverse a tree was one of the most fun Data Structures related experiences I've had. It really shows you how recursion can go from being an intimidating thing to feeling simple. After I did this project I had to make an active effort to STOP thinking recursively. Another bonus of this project was that I got to actually use the HashMap system I'd built. JavaScript has one built in sure, but it feels good to build your own tools - at least to learn how they really work.
